# Community-Led Growth (Grassroots)

As a developer, you don't need a huge Google Ads budget. You need deep penetration in hyper-niche communities.

## 1. Infiltrating Reddit
Do not spam links to your app. You will get banned. Provide value first.
- **In `r/Strava`:** Wait for someone to complain about the Strava subscription price hike. Reply with: *"Yeah this frustrated me so much I actually spent the last 6 months coding an alternative with better data visualization. It's totally free right now if anyone wants to join the alpha."*
- **In `r/running`:** Post a highly technical breakdown of how GPS watch metrics vary from phone GPS, using data from your own app to prove the point. At the bottom, casually drop the app link.

## 2. Leveraging Strava Clubs
- If users can authenticate via OAuth (which we planned), identify running club leaders who use your app.
- Give club leaders "Premium" for free. Provide them with tools that Strava doesn't have (like the Volunteer Management System or QR Code check-ins we brainstormed).
- If the club leader moves their group logistics to RunHub, 50-100 runners will follow them immediately.

## 3. Local Run Clubs (Do Things That Don't Scale)
- Actually go to a local running club in your city.
- Tell them you are a local developer building a platform.
- Ask if they will let you host a "RunHub 5K Time Trial" at the local track. Buy $50 worth of pizza, require them to download the app to get their official chip time. You instantly acquire 40 hyper-local, highly engaged users.
