# The Go-To-Market (GTM) Launch Plan

You cannot launch to crickets. You need a phased rollout to build momentum.

## Phase 1: The Closed Alpha (Next 30 Days)
**Goal:** 50 highly engaged runners to stress-test your backend.
- **Where to find them:** Local running clubs, your personal network, posting a beta-testing invite in `r/running` or `r/advancedrunning` on Reddit.
- **The Pitch:** "I'm a solo dev building a Strava alternative with a built-in custom graphical export studio. I need 50 runners to break it."
- **Feedback Loop:** Create a private Discord or WhatsApp group. Let them vote on features directly.

## Phase 2: Open Beta & Indie Launch (Month 2-3)
**Goal:** 500-1,000 users.
- **ProductHunt Launch:** Create a polished launch video. Emphasize the **Activity Canvas/Export Studio** because that's what makes it visually distinct from Strava.
- **HackerNews (Show HN):** Developers love technical breakdowns. Write a post called: *Show HN: I built a real-time GPS tracking and activity canvas app using Angular and Spring Boot.*
- **Twitter/X Thread:** "I was tired of Strava's boring UI, so I built my own running platform. Here are the 5 hardest architectural problems I solved..."

## Phase 3: The Virality Engine Activation
**Goal:** 5,000+ users via organic loops.
- Turn on standard user registration.
- Launch the Community Leaderboards.
- Ensure every social media export shared by users has a scannable QR code watermark linking directly to the App Store/Play Store download page.
