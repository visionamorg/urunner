# Brand & Influencer Outreach (B2B Marketing)

Once you reach ~5,000 active users, your marketing strategy shifts. You can use your user base to attract brands, which in turn attracts more users.

## 1. The Strategy: Give Brands What They Want (Data)
We brainstormed the **"Brand ROI Dashboard."** This is your trojan horse.
- Traditional running influencers cost brands $1,000 for a sweaty selfie that gets 5,000 views but 0 trackable sales.
- **Your Pitch to Salomon/Nike/Tracksmith:** "Sponsor a challenge on RunHub. I will give you a dashboard showing exactly how many kilometers your demographic ran, and how many of them clicked your 20% discount code."

## 2. Cold Emailing Brand Managers
Find "Community Marketing Manager" or "Field Marketing Specialist" at running brands on LinkedIn.

**The Email Template:**
> Hi [Name], I'm the developer of RunHub, a fast-growing running app with [X,000] active users. 
> Strava charges brands $25,000 minimum to host a sponsored challenge. I'm building out our brand infrastructure and want to offer [Brand Name] our first sponsored challenge entirely for free. 
> We will put your logo on our Export Studio templates for 1 month, generating thousands of Instagram shares natively. 
> Let me know if you are open to a 10-minute demo of tracking dashboard.

## 3. Micro-Influencer Affiliate Program
Find runners on Instagram/TikTok with 2,000 - 10,000 followers (they run fast but aren't famous).
Give them a custom "Export Studio" template that has their name on it.
Every time someone joins the app using their referral code, give them a free month of Premium or a physical piece of merch. Developers build systems; this is an automated referral system.
