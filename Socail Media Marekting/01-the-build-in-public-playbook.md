# The "Build in Public" Social Media Playbook

As a developer, your best social media strategy is extreme transparency. People connect with the *creator*, not just the product.

## Where to Post
- **Twitter/X:** The hub for \#buildinpublic, indie hackers, and tech enthusiasts.
- **LinkedIn:** Incredible organic reach for professional milestones, architectural deep-dives, and B2B growth.
- **TikTok/Reels:** For showing the beautiful visual exports of the app directly in action.

## Content Pillars (What to Tweet/Post About)
1. **The Wins:** "Just crossed 100 active runners today! Here is the exact tech stack I used to handle all 100 simultaneous GPS streams."
2. **The Failures (Highly Engaging):** "I accidentally deleted the production database for my running app today at 3 AM. Here is how I recovered the user workouts using raw server logs. A lesson in proper backup architecture."
3. **The Teasers:** Post a video of the Export Studio UI. "I got sick of normal Strava maps, so I coded an interactive 3D fly-through for my running app. Shipping next week. Thoughts?"
4. **The Financials:** Show your Stripe dashboard. "RunHub just hit $100 MRR. It covers server costs. The dream is real."

**Why this works:** You aren't "selling" an app. You are inviting people to watch a documentary of a developer building something cool. They download the app to support the protagonist.
