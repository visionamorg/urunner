# The Developer's Weekly Social Media Calendar

You don't have time to be a full-time social media manager. You need to write backend code. 
Here is a lightweight, 30-minutes-a-week schedule tailored for a solo dev.

## Monday: The Technical Drop (Twitter/LinkedIn)
- **Topic:** Share a snippet of code, an architectural diagram, or a bug you fixed over the weekend.
- **Goal:** Build authority as a talented engineer. Attract the indie-hacker/tech crowd. 
- *Time required: 10 mins.*

## Wednesday: The Visual Teaser (TikTok/Instagram Reel)
- **Topic:** Post a 5-second aesthetic video of the app UI working in the real world (e.g., a screen recording of the map tracking you while you jog).
- **Goal:** Product awareness. Show, don't tell.
- *Time required: 15 mins.*

## Friday: The Community Question (Twitter/Discord)
- **Topic:** Ask a polarizing or interesting question to your audience to drive engagement.
- **Examples:** 
  - "Designing the RunHub export canvases. Which font layout do you prefer, A or B?"
  - "Runners, what's one feature Garmin watches have that you wish was just an app on your phone?"
- **Goal:** Engagement farming and free product feedback.
- *Time required: 5 mins.*

## Automation Tips
- Use a tool like **Buffer** or **Typefully** on Sunday night to schedule all three posts for the week. 
- You do not need to post every day. Consistency (3x a week) beats burning out for 2 weeks in a row.
