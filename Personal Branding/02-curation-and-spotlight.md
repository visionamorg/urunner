# The "Community Spotlight" Strategy (Curation)

You mentioned wanting to "impersonate" users to show off the app. We need to be careful with the word *impersonate*—faking user data can backfire. Instead, use a **Curation Strategy**.

## The Execution: Featuring Real Runners
Instead of faking fake runners, take the public runs of actual runners in your city or network, pump their data through your Export Studio, and feature them.

1. **Find the Target:** Find an active runner on Instagram (e.g., someone with 1k-5k followers in your city).
2. **Scrape the Data (Ethically):** Look at a run they just posted on Strava or their story. They ran 10km at a 5:30 pace in the local park.
3. **Generate the Canvas:** Go into your RunHub development environment. Input their 10km stats, use a photo they took (or a generic beautiful photo of that park), and generate the "Clear Info Card" template. 
4. **Post to Your Story:** Post it to your 2,300 followers with the caption: *"Massive 10k effort today by @target_username! 🏃‍♂️🔥 Visualized with RunHub."*

## Why This Works (The Viral Loop)
When you tag someone on an Instagram Story, they get a direct message (DM). 
Because your graphic looks incredibly premium and professional out of the Export Studio, **they will almost always tap "Add This To My Story".**

Suddenly, your app's branded aesthetic graphic is pushed to *their* 2,000 followers, acting as a free endorsement for your brand.
